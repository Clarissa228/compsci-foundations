# What is Computer Science?

## The Big Idea

Computer Science is **not** just about coding. It's the study of *problem solving* — figuring out how to take a complex problem and break it down into steps a machine can follow.

Think of it this way: a recipe is a kind of program. It takes ingredients (inputs), follows a sequence of instructions, and produces a dish (output). CS is about writing those instructions precisely and efficiently.

---

## What CS Actually Covers

CS is a huge field. Here are the main branches:

- **Programming** — Writing instructions for computers in languages like Python, Java, or C
- **Algorithms** — Finding the best sequence of steps to solve a problem
- **Data Structures** — Organizing data so it can be used efficiently
- **Systems** — How hardware, operating systems, and networks actually work
- **Artificial Intelligence** — Teaching machines to learn and make decisions
- **Databases** — Storing and retrieving large amounts of information
- **Theory** — The mathematics behind what computers *can* and *cannot* do

This course will touch all of these areas.

---

## What is a Program?

A **program** is a set of instructions written in a language a computer can understand. Computers are fast but dumb — they do *exactly* what you tell them, nothing more.

Here's a super simple Python program:

```python
name = input("What's your name? ")
print("Hello, " + name + "!")
```

That's it. Two lines. The computer reads your name and says hello. Everything in CS, from social media algorithms to self-driving cars, is built from small steps like these.

---

## Why Learn CS?

- It teaches you to think logically and break big problems into small ones
- It's one of the most in-demand and well-paid skill sets in the world
- It powers everything from your phone to space telescopes
- It can be applied to *any* field — medicine, music, finance, biology
